1. Cards

There is a number of each rank of cards (1 through 12) equal to that rank, so twelve 12s, eleven 11s, and so on. There are also two Jokers, which are wild cards.

There are 80 cards in total.

2. Rules

>
>
>

The rule is simple. For example, if a first player submitted three 12s cards, following players should submit three cards smaller than previous one. If a first player paid two cards, others also should pay two cards smaller than former one. In other words, you can put only cards that are same number as previous cards and have less rank than given ones. (Jokers are special. They play wildcard role only when given with different cards. However, Jokers have rank 13 when they are alone.)

If you don't have cards satisfying above rules or don't want to put them now, you can just pass that turn.

3. Taxation

The rank of players is also important. There are several classes in this game.

  • The Greater Dalmuti
  • The Lesser Dalmuti
  • The Commoner
  • The Lesser Beggar
  • The Greater Beggar

In this situation, beggars should pay their best cards to dalmuties as tax. Dalmuties also give their worst cards to beggars. They exchange their cards each other before starting game.

Does it seem unfair? Yes, but that is the previlege of The King and The Nobility, and that is the rule.
